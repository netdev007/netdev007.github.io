Scripting

Greeting

“Good (morning/afternoon/evening), my name is (__________) from AT&T Tech Support 360.”

“May I speak with Mr./Ms. (Member’s last name)?”

Important Note: If the person who answers is not the Member on the account, but confirms that the appointment is scheduled for him/her, ask for his/her first and last name.

“Mr./Ms. (last name) I’m calling for our scheduled appointment, but before we proceed I want to let you know this call will be recorded for quality and training purposes.”

If the customer would rather not be recorded:

“If you would prefer to conduct your appointment through our remote connectivity software only I can send you instructions via email on how to connect for today’s appointment. The email address I have on file is ___________. Is that where you would like me to send the instructions?”

1.      Open an internet browser and go to http://tech360help.com
2.      Type the first and last name on the account into the box and click “Continue”
3.      In the “Please enter your name” box type your first and last name, then click “Submit”
4.      You will get a message inside the chat box that states, “<TECH NAME> is requesting to elevate to full remote support which will allow more features like screen sharing and file transfer. You will be required to run an application that will be sent to you.  Do you wish to continue?"

Note: By clicking “Accept” on this message you are agreeing to our terms of service. If you would like to review the terms of service you can click on the link that says, “Full Terms and Conditions”. Once you are ready to proceed, click “Accept”.

 5.      Click “Run” on the file download box
6.      You may also get a prompts from your operating system and/or security software requesting to grant the program “Bomgar” permission to run. If so, please select the necessary option to allow the software.
7.      Once you see, “<TECH NAME>can now view and control the customer's screen” you should be all set.

“I have all the information that I need to work on your issue. Since it will take some time to resolve your issue, rather than keeping you on the phone is it OK if I call you back when I am done?

(Wait for customer)

"You can always contact me via the onscreen chat if you need to reach me. It should take approximately (provide expected call back time frame). Thank you for choosing AT&T Tech Support 360, where we value your business.”

Transfer to another department

“The issue you are experiencing is handled by another department. I will need to transfer you to that department so that we can resolve this for you. Would you like me to transfer you now? (Wait for customer response). Before I transfer you, let me provide you with the direct number for that department in case we get disconnected.”

Pre-Closing: Pushing Scripts & Survey

“On our appointment today we completed (explain what you did). Is there anything else that I can do for you?”

IF NO:
“Let me provide you with a Service Ticket number for today’s appointment in case you need to call us back regarding this issue. (Provide Service Ticket ID).”

IF YES:
“I will be creating a new Service Ticket to address your additional issues. Before I proceed let me provide you with a Service Ticket number for the issues we have already discussed in case you need to call us back regarding those. (Provide Service Ticket ID).”

Once the Service Ticket ID has been provided, proceed with the rest of the script.

“I will push a script to you now. This states that you don’t need any more assistance today. Please agree that the work has been completed by typing “No” in the chat window.”

Note: Wait for the customer to reply.

“When I disconnect from your system you will receive a brief survey, I would appreciate you taking the time to fill it out so my manager knows how I’m doing. I hope the support I've provided today has earned a 'Very Satisfied' rating as well as a 10 indicating you would recommend our services to others."
